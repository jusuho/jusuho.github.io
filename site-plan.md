## Positioning

This site should present Zhao Shou Hao as an engineering leader rather than a generic software portfolio candidate.

Core positioning:
- Senior Engineering Leader with Head/Director potential
- 7+ years of software, system integration, and delivery experience
- Strong in team building, architecture, delivery systems, governance, and business-facing execution
- Able to translate ambiguity into operating cadence, technical direction, and measurable business outcomes

Why this positioning:
- The strongest signal in the resume is not isolated coding skill
- The strongest signal is leading teams, driving architecture change, and turning technical work into operational impact
- To attract higher-level hiring managers, the site must show not only execution strength but also organizational judgment, prioritization ability, and executive communication readiness

Executive opportunity goal:
- Increase credibility for `Head of Engineering`, `Director of Engineering`, `Engineering Manager`, and `Technical Lead` opportunities
- Make CEOs, CTOs, and GMs quickly see that this profile can own both delivery and organizational scale-up
- Position the candidate as someone who can grow from current technical leadership into broader engineering ownership

Positioning guardrail:
- Do not overclaim titles such as VP or CTO if the resume evidence is not yet strong enough
- Instead, present a credible upward trajectory: already operating beyond individual contribution, with evidence of leadership scope and readiness for larger ownership

## Writing Direction

Avoid weak resume phrases such as:
- Passionate about learning
- Love challenges
- Enthusiastic about technology

Prefer direct business and leadership framing such as:
- Built and led engineering teams
- Drove architecture modernization
- Improved delivery speed and engineering quality
- Connected technical work to operational outcomes
- Reduced delivery risk and operating cost
- Created systems, standards, and decision frameworks that scale teams

Executive writing rules:
- Lead with business consequence before tool names
- Prefer verbs like `led`, `owned`, `drove`, `standardized`, `scaled`, `reduced`, `aligned`
- Avoid listing too many technologies in the first screen
- Treat technology as supporting evidence, not the headline
- Every major claim should ideally answer one of these:
  - Did revenue, cost, speed, quality, or risk improve?
  - Did team capability or organizational leverage increase?
  - Why would an executive trust this person with larger scope?

Important credibility rule:
- Remove or qualify metrics that are not confidently defensible
- For example, if `提升 Sprint 交付速率 30%` is not rigorously measured, rewrite as `顯著提升交付速度` or explain the basis
